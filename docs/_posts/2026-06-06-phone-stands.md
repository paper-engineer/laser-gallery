knocked this out because it was sitting on my brain and I wanted it for tomorrow. learned a lot about processing images, in between waiting for inkscape to compute. also force-closed inkscape from the task manager like 10 times. and downloaded my first extension, which led to 3 entire pc restarts. 

Material: 3mm pine ply

Time: anywhere from 4 minutes (easy phone stands with easy line engraving) to 40+ minutes (the mountain one)

Settings: left the focus where it was, I think about 10mm. cut at 600/100, fill engrave at uhh I think it was 8000/20, line engrave at 2000/20, line art engrave at 4000/20. 

First I made the test pieces, which is how I figured out that 2000/20 was a bit much for the art style I was putting in. Then I made the pieces I was after, and that's how I figured out that you still need two lines at 4000/20 to make a decent outline. And that line art takes long anyway. 

It is a much different style, but timing-wise, the line-artsy mountain phone stand came out pretty similar to the fill engraved phone stand in laser time. Lightburn's estimated job time barely changed at all between 8000/40 and 4000/20, and the way I figure that is, it's probably because line art doesn't let the laser head accelerate at all so it's not like I ever actually get to that speed. 

oh, and for some reason the basic phone stand isn't a closed shape? also remind me to adjust the hole a bit thicker next time, squishing it this time was no fun at all. all files need cleanup, apparently.
